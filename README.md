## üìñ Abstract

This study investigates the psychosocial factors influencing caffeine consumption among young adults in Vietnam, specifically focusing on university students aged 15‚Äì24 living in the University Village area (Ho Chi Minh City).

Using a **mixed-methods approach** (Quantitative $n=142$ & Qualitative Focus Group), we applied the **Theory of Planned Behavior (TPB)** to analyze the impact of *Health Awareness, Academic Stress, and Peer Influence* on consumption habits.

## üéØ Key Hypotheses & Findings

| Hypothesis | Relationship | Result | Key Insight |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **H1** | Health Awareness $\rightarrow$ Intention | ‚ùå **Rejected** | Students often ignore health risks when consuming caffeine. |
| **H2** | Academic Stress $\rightarrow$ Intention | ‚úÖ **Accepted** | High pressure leads to higher caffeine craving. |
| **H3** | Peer Influence $\rightarrow$ Intention | ‚úÖ **Accepted** | "Coffee culture" and socializing are major drivers. |
| **H4** | Intention $\rightarrow$ Actual Behavior | ‚úÖ **Accepted** | Strong correlation between planning and drinking. |
| **H5** | Gender Moderation | ‚ùå **Rejected** | Males and females show similar consumption patterns. |
